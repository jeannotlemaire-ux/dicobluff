# Product

## Register

product

## Users

French-speaking players aged roughly 18–40 who enjoy vocabulary and social deception games. Context ranges from solo play (commute, downtime) to group sessions with friends (table or remote). The solo mode puts them against AI literary characters; the multiplayer mode is a shared social experience. Players range from casual word enthusiasts to people who genuinely enjoy dictionaries.

## Product Purpose

Dicobluff is a word-bluffing game (Balderdash style) entirely in French. A rare word is displayed; players invent plausible fake definitions to fool opponents, then vote for the real one. Scoring rewards both convincing bluffs and correctly identifying the true definition. Success looks like: a player smiling when their fake definition fools an AI opponent, or a group laughing at an absurd definition a friend wrote.

## Brand Personality

Joueur · Vif · Intellectuel — fun-first and quick-paced, but grounded in genuine lexical culture. The literary AI characters (Dantès, Don Juan, Esmeralda…) give the game a theatrical, slightly elevated register. Wit over whimsy; intelligence over trivia-show energy.

## Anti-references

- **Duolingo / gamification overload**: no streak mascots, no confetti spam, no hyper-cheerful reward loops. XP and progression exist but should feel understated.
- **Trivia app aesthetics**: no bright primary colors, no chunky answer buttons, no quiz-show vibe. The game is literary bluffing, not a pub quiz.

## Design Principles

1. **Literary wit over gamification noise** — every UI element should feel like it could belong in an elegant literary salon, not a mobile game store.
2. **Speed serves the bluff** — interactions must be fast enough that the social tension doesn't die. Transitions and reveals should feel snappy, not performative.
3. **Confidence, not hand-holding** — the interface trusts the player. No tooltips explaining obvious things, no excessive confirmation dialogs.
4. **Characters are the soul** — the AI opponents (Dantès, Esmeralda…) carry personality. Their voices and reactions should feel distinct, not interchangeable.
5. **Dark and focused** — the deep marine background keeps attention on words and definitions. Color is used purposefully: gold for primary actions, coral for stakes, teal for progress.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

- Target WCAG 2.1 AA for all interactive elements.
- Reduced motion already handled in CSS (`prefers-reduced-motion`).
- Focus-visible styles defined for keyboard navigation.
- French language only (no i18n planned).
- No known specific user accommodation requirements beyond standard AA compliance.
